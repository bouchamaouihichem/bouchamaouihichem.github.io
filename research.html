"Search for boosted Higgs decays to a pair of spin-0 particles in the bbbb final state in Run2" (NPS-25-005, CERN link)

People involved: Jim Olsen, Andrew Brinkerhoff

  • Developed analysis strategy and framework of all leptonic categories.
  • Led studies about lepton trigger, ID and isolation to optimize the selection of leptonic categories.
  • Evaluated muon scale factors and systematic uncertainties for custom working points using the Muon POG Tag and Probe (TnP) tool.
  • Led studies about the topology of a boosted Higgs boson and a back-to-back Z/W boson and reweighted signal events accordingly.
  • Collaborated with the Baylor group to produce expected limits on H→aa branching ratio with the 2D-Alphabet algorithm using a custom ParticleNet tagger and pseudoscalar mass regressor.

"Search for exotic decays of the Higgs boson to a pair of pseudoscalars in the μμbb and ττbb final states" (HIG-22-007, public link)

People involved: Jim Olsen, Pallabi Das

  • Trained and optimized a ParticleNet tagger for 2 b-quarks coming from a Higgs decaying to two pseudoscalars decaying to b quarks and τ pairs.
  • Generated several low pseudoscalar mass signal samples to improve tagger performance.
  • Presented results showing ParticleNet bb-tagger outperformed standard CMS b/bb taggers.

"DeepCore 2.0: Convolutional Neural Network for Tracking in Jets with High Transverse Momentum" (CMS-DP-2024/003, public link)

People involved: Jim Olsen, Slava Krutelyov, Marco Musich

  • Updated DeepCore 1.0 to DeepCore 2.0 with Run 3 samples, CNN architecture improvements, and hyperparameter tuning.
  • Developed DeepCore 2.0-Hybrid with JetCore, improving tracking efficiency by ~10% for pT > 100 GeV while maintaining low fake rate and lowering CPU usage.
  • Validated model using new CMS detector configuration Monte Carlo samples.
  • Integrated DeepCore 2.0-Hybrid into CMSSW for use in 2024 data taking and simulation.
  • Supervised ongoing optimization efforts for Phase-2 LHC.
